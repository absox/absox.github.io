Feeds four. Masses and volumes are approximate and may require adjustment. This is a vegetarian dumpling recipe of my own invention, refined through trial and error. This version has proven most popular with my friends, even those who eat meat.

Ingredients:

Wrappers

  • About two cups of flour
  • Water, the exact quantity may vary depending on conditions.

Filling:

  • 2 packs of dried pressed tofu (five spice flavor)
  • 3-4 medium zucchini
  • Half a nappa cabbage.
  • Quarter pound of shiitake mushrooms.
  • Ginger, one tablespoon, adjust to taste.
  • Soy sauce, a liberal quantity, adjust to taste.
  • Sesame oil, a generous drizzle.
  • Two whole eggs (can be omitted for vegans).
  • Salt and pepper to taste.

Instructions:

  1. Mix flour and water until you have a soft, homogeneous dough. Knead for at least 15 minutes, then cover with plastic wrap and let rest while you prepare the other ingredients.
  2. Finely mince the filling ingredients. Combine in a bowl and mix well.
  3. Generous flour a surface and roll dumpling skins thinly with a rolling pin (dowel, no handles, untapered).
  4. Fill dumplings, making sure skins are rolled thinly, and each dumpling is filled to capacity to avoid too thick of a skin at any point. This takes some manual dexterity, and the skill to do so can't really be conveyed through text. I recommend some Chinese cooking Youtube channels.
  5. Boil in water in a pot without crowding, around 3 minutes after reaching a rolling boil.
  6. Serve and enjoy. I recommend Weichuan hot dumpling sauce.
